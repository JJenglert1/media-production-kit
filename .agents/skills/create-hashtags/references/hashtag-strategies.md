# Hashtag Strategies

Research-backed hashtag selection and optimization strategies. Platform-specific tactics for maximum discoverability.

## The Pyramid Strategy

The most reliable hashtag approach: mix hashtag "tiers" by post volume to balance reach and competition.

### Tiers

| Tier | Post Volume | Competition | Engagement Rate | Use Per Post |
|------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Broad | >1M posts | Extreme — content buried in minutes | Low (high volume dilutes) | 1-2 max |
| Medium | 100K-1M posts | Moderate — discoverable sweet spot | Medium | 1-2 |
| Niche | <100K posts | Low — engaged communities | High (60-70% higher than broad) | 1-2 |
| Branded | Varies | None (owned by you) | Highest (direct audience) | 0-1 |

### Recommended Pyramid Mix

5 hashtags (YouTube, Instagram, LinkedIn):
- 1 Broad + 2 Medium + 2 Niche
- OR: 1 Broad + 1 Medium + 2 Niche + 1 Branded

2 hashtags (Twitter/X):
- 1 Medium + 1 Niche
- OR: 1 Trending + 1 Niche

7-9 hashtags (TikTok extended):
- 2 Broad + 3 Medium + 3 Niche + 1 Branded (3-3-3 strategy)

## The 3-3-3 Strategy (TikTok)

Specifically designed for TikTok's algorithm:

1. 3 Broad/High-Volume: General category tags (e.g., #Marketing, #Cooking, #Fitness)
2. 3 Niche/Community: Specific community tags (e.g., #SocialMediaManager, #VeganMealPrep)
3. 3 Content-Specific: Tags describing exactly what this specific post is about (e.g., #HowToWriteCopy, #30MinuteDinner)

Why it works: Broad tags signal the category, niche tags find the right audience, content-specific tags match search intent. TikTok's algorithm uses all three signals.

## Trending vs Evergreen Balance

### Trending Hashtags
- Short shelf life, massive reach potential
- Best for: timely content, news, events, seasonal topics
- Risk: content becomes irrelevant quickly; high competition
- Source: platform Explore/Trending tabs, social media trend trackers

### Evergreen Hashtags
- Consistent performance over months/years
- Best for: educational content, tutorials, how-tos, cornerstone content
- Advantage: reliable, steady traffic without expiration
- Examples: #ProductivityTips, #HealthyRecipes, #CodingTips

### Recommended Mix
- 60-70% evergreen — stable foundation
- 30-40% trending — discovery boost
- Rotate trending tags regularly; keep evergreen tags consistent

## Platform-Specific Tactics

### YouTube
- First 3 matter most — they appear as clickable links above the video title
- Shorts: Put hashtags in the title (after main text), max 2 in title
- Long-form: Put hashtags at the end of the description
- Title vs Description: Title hashtags have stronger search impact
- Never exceed 15 — YouTube ignores ALL hashtags if you go over

### Instagram
- Caption preferred — hashtags in captions increase reach by ~36% vs first comment
- First comment — viable alternative for larger accounts (keeps caption clean)
- Keyword search era — Instagram now uses caption keywords for search, not just hashtags
- Rotate sets — using identical hashtags on every post gets flagged as spammy
- Banned hashtag check — always verify hashtags aren't on Instagram's banned list

### TikTok
- Search engine behavior — users search TikTok like Google; hashtags = SEO keywords
- Audio-caption alignment — hashtags matching words spoken in your video AND in your caption get algorithmic boost
- Niche tags drive conversion — broad tags get views, niche tags get followers
- #FYP debate — #fyp has 1.7T+ views but intense competition; use as 1 of 5-9, never alone

### Twitter/X
- 1-2 is the hard rule — engagement drops 17% with 3+ hashtags
- Placement matters:
  - Mid-tweet (best): "This #ContentStrategy tip boosted our engagement 55%"
  - End-of-tweet (acceptable): "The secret to viral threads #ContentTips"
  - Start-of-tweet (never): "#ContentTips are important" — algorithm penalizes this
- Branded + trending pair — one branded hashtag + one relevant trending tag

### LinkedIn
- SEO function — LinkedIn removed hashtag following; hashtags now function as search keywords
- PascalCase mandatory — accessibility requirement; screen readers depend on it
- Professional tone — use industry-standard terms, not casual/trendy language
- Placement: Always at the bottom of the post, separated from content

## Hashtag Research Methodology

### Step 1: Seed List
Start with 3-5 obvious hashtags related to the content topic.

### Step 2: Competitor Analysis
- Find 5-10 top creators in the niche
- Note which hashtags their best-performing posts use
- Identify patterns: which tags appear repeatedly among top performers?

### Step 3: Related Tag Expansion
- Search each seed hashtag on the target platform
- Look at "Related hashtags" or "Similar tags" suggestions
- Expand the list to 15-20 candidates

### Step 4: Tier Classification
- Check post volume for each candidate (broad >1M, medium 100K-1M, niche <100K)
- Verify engagement quality: do posts using this tag actually get engagement?
- A niche tag with active engagement beats a broad tag where everything gets buried

### Step 5: Safety Check
- Cross-reference against known banned hashtag lists
- Check for unintended word combinations in concatenated hashtags
- Verify the tag is associated with content similar to yours (no embarrassing mismatches)

### Step 6: Select & Organize
- Choose the final set respecting the platform's optimal count
- Arrange in pyramid format (broad → medium → niche)
- Ensure PascalCase formatting

## Branded Hashtag Strategy

### When to Create a Branded Hashtag
- Consistent content series (e.g., #MondayMotivationWith[Name])
- Community building (e.g., #[BrandName]Community)
- Campaign tracking (e.g., #[BrandName]Challenge)

### Rules for Branded Hashtags
- Keep it short and memorable (under 20 characters)
- Make it unique — check that it's not already in use for something else
- Always include it consistently across posts (builds recognition over time)
- Encourage audience use by resharing UGC that uses your branded tag

## Common Hashtag Anti-Patterns

1. Copy-pasting identical sets — rotate 5-10 different sets by content theme
2. All broad tags — high competition, low engagement per post
3. Irrelevant trending tags — algorithms detect content-hashtag mismatch
4. Exceeding platform limits — YouTube ignores ALL if >15; Twitter engagement drops with >2
5. Lowercase only — inaccessible to screen readers; always use PascalCase
6. Long phrase tags — #HowToMakeTheBestPancakesEver is unreadable; prefer #BestPancakeRecipe
7. Banned tags — Instagram hides posts using banned hashtags from non-followers
8. No rotation — identical hashtag blocks on every post signals spam behavior
