# Title Formulas

Proven fill-in-the-blank title templates. Each formula targets a different psychological trigger. Mix formulas across variations for maximum A/B testing value.

## Formula 1: How-To

Pattern: `How to [Achieve Result] in [Timeframe] [Bracket]`

Trigger: Practical value — "I can learn to do this"

Examples:
- "How to Edit Videos Like a Pro in 10 Minutes"
- "How to Double Your Revenue Without Ads [2026]"
- "How to Build a Website From Scratch [Step-by-Step]"

Variants:
- "How I [Achieved Result] in [Timeframe]" (personal story angle)
- "How to [Result] (Even If You're a Complete Beginner)"
- "The Fastest Way to [Achieve Result]"

Best for: Tutorials, educational content, skill-building, productivity.

## Formula 2: Listicle / Number

Pattern: `[Odd Number] [Category] That [Benefit/Surprise]`

Trigger: Structured, scannable promise — "I know exactly what I'm getting"

Examples:
- "7 Camera Settings Most Beginners Get Wrong"
- "11 VS Code Extensions That Will Change Your Life"
- "5 Morning Habits of Millionaires [Science-Backed]"

Variants:
- "[Number] Mistakes [Group] Makes (And How to Fix Them)"
- "[Number] [Things] You Didn't Know About [Topic]"
- "Top [Number] [Category] for [Year/Use Case]"

Rules:
- Odd numbers outperform even by ~20% CTR
- 5, 7, and 11 are the strongest performers
- Specific numbers ("$437") outperform round numbers ("$400")

Best for: Tips, roundups, reviews, rankings, list content.

## Formula 3: Question

Pattern: `[Provocative Question] + [Context/Stakes]`

Trigger: Cognitive engagement — the brain automatically tries to answer

Examples:
- "Is YouTube Still Worth Starting in 2026?"
- "Why Are Programmers Switching to This Language?"
- "Can You Really Learn Piano in 30 Days?"

Variants:
- "What Happens When You [Do Extreme Thing]?"
- "Why Does Nobody Talk About [Topic]?"
- "Is [Common Belief] Actually True?"

Best for: Debates, opinions, myth-busting, trend analysis, reviews.

## Formula 4: Challenge / Experiment

Pattern: `I [Did Extreme Thing] for [Duration] — Here's What Happened`

Trigger: Vicarious experience — "I want to see the result without doing it myself"

Examples:
- "I Lived on $1 a Day for 30 Days"
- "I Quit Social Media for 90 Days — Here's What Changed"
- "I Built an App in 24 Hours With No Code"

Variants:
- "I Tried [Thing] So You Don't Have To"
- "I Followed [Expert's] Advice for [Duration] — Was It Worth It?"
- "[Duration] of [Extreme Thing]: The Results Will Surprise You"

Best for: Vlogs, personal experiments, product tests, lifestyle content.

## Formula 5: Versus / Comparison

Pattern: `[Thing A] vs [Thing B] — [Definitive Claim or Surprising Angle]`

Trigger: Decision support — "help me choose" + curiosity about the winner

Examples:
- "$10 vs $1000 Camera — Can You Tell the Difference?"
- "React vs Svelte in 2026 — Which Should You Learn?"
- "Cheap vs Expensive [Product] — Honest Comparison [DATA]"

Variants:
- "[Option A] or [Option B]? The REAL Answer"
- "I Tested [Both] — One Is Clearly Better"
- "[Thing] at Every Price Point: $10, $100, $1000"

Best for: Product reviews, tech comparisons, debates, buying guides.

## Formula 6: Contrarian / Myth-Busting

Pattern: `Why [Common Belief] Is Wrong` or `Stop [Common Advice] (Do THIS Instead)`

Trigger: Pattern interrupt — challenges what the viewer thinks they know

Examples:
- "Why Waking Up at 5AM Is Destroying Your Health"
- "Stop Using useEffect for THIS (Do This Instead)"
- "Everything You Know About [Topic] Is Wrong"

Variants:
- "The [Topic] Advice That's Actually Hurting You"
- "[Number] [Niche] Myths That Need to Die"
- "Unpopular Opinion: [Bold Claim About Topic]"

Warning: Must deliver genuine insight — don't be contrarian just for clicks.

Best for: Expert content, educational, opinion pieces, myth-busting.

## Formula 7: Result / Transformation

Pattern: `[Starting State] → [End State] in [Timeframe]`

Trigger: Aspiration — "I want that transformation too"

Examples:
- "From 0 to 100K Subscribers: The Exact Strategy"
- "I Went From Broke to $10K/Month — Here's How"
- "Complete Beginner to Full-Stack Developer in 6 Months"

Variants:
- "How [Specific Person/I] Went From [Bad] to [Good]"
- "[Before] vs [After]: My [Topic] Journey"
- "The [Number]-Step System That Took Me From [A] to [B]"

Best for: Success stories, case studies, course/program promotions, personal growth.

## Formula 8: Urgency / FOMO

Pattern: `[Action] Before [Deadline/Consequence]` or `[Thing] You NEED to Know Before [Event]`

Trigger: Fear of missing out — "I need to act now"

Examples:
- "Watch This Before You Buy a MacBook in 2026"
- "The AI Tool Everyone's Using (Before It Goes Paid)"
- "Last Chance: [Thing] Is About to Change Forever"

Variants:
- "Don't [Action] Until You Watch This"
- "[Number] Things to Do Before [Event/Deadline]"
- "Why You Need to Start [Action] NOW"

Warning: Only use genuine urgency. Fake urgency erodes trust.

Best for: News, launches, time-sensitive content, buying advice, trend content.

## Formula Selection Guide

| Content Type | Primary Formula | Strong Alternative |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Tutorial | How-To | Listicle |
| Tips/Advice | Listicle | Contrarian |
| Review | Versus | Question |
| Vlog/Personal | Challenge | Result/Transformation |
| Opinion | Contrarian | Question |
| News/Trends | Urgency | Question |
| Case Study | Result/Transformation | How-To |
| Comparison | Versus | Listicle |
| Myth-busting | Contrarian | Listicle |
| Entertainment | Challenge | Question |

## Combining Formulas

Strong titles often blend two formulas:

- Listicle + Contrarian: "7 'Healthy' Foods That Are Actually Terrible for You"
- How-To + Result: "How I Made $10K in 30 Days With This Simple System"
- Question + Challenge: "Can a Beginner Beat a Pro Chef? I Tried."
- Versus + Urgency: "iPhone 17 vs Samsung S27 — Watch Before You Buy [2026]"
